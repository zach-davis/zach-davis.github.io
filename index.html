Biography

Zach Davis is a PhD student in cognitive science at NYU, supervised by Bob Rehder. His research involves using computational methods to study how limited agents (i.e. people) are able to grapple with our complex world. These interests have led to projects investigating how people control dynamic environments, learn the structure of chaotic systems as they unfold in real time, and make scalable causal judgments.

Selected Publications

A Process Model of Causal Cognition

Proposed a variant of the Metropolis-Hastings sampling algorithm as a rational process model for causal inference across a wide variety of tasks.
ZJ Davis, BE Rehder
Under review, 2018

Causal Structure Learning with Continuous Variables in Continuous Time

We present a formal system of causal relationships using Ornstein-Uhlenbeck processes, finding that people use limited representations to deal with the vastly denser information flow.
ZJ Davis, NR Bramley, BE Rehder
In CogSci Proceedings, 2018

A Causal Model Approach to Dynamic Control

Developed a testbed for complex dynamic control tasks, finding that people’s behavior closely matched the predictions of an optimal controller and deep-Q network.
